Container Sharing allows you to collaborate with other Hearth users by sharing your containers and their contents. Whether you're managing a household inventory with family members, organizing shared spaces with roommates, or coordinating supplies with team members, container sharing makes collaboration simple and secure.

## ğŸš€ Getting Started

### Prerequisites
- You must be an approved Hearth user
- The person you want to share with must also be approved
- You must be the owner of the container you want to share

### Quick Start
1. **Navigate** to any container you own
2. **Click** the blue "Share" button
3. **Enter** the email address of the person you want to share with
4. **Select** their permission level
5. **Click** "Share Container"

That's it! The other person will immediately have access to your container.

## ğŸ“± How to Share a Container

### Step 1: Access the Share Feature
1. **Open** the container you want to share
2. **Look** for the blue "Share" button in the top-right area
3. **Click** the Share button to open the sharing modal

*Note: You'll only see the Share button if you own the container or have Admin permission.*

### Step 2: Find the User
1. **Type** the email address of the person you want to share with
2. **Wait** for the system to find and validate the user
3. **Confirm** the correct person appears (you'll see their name and email)

*Note: The person must be an approved Hearth user. If they're not found, they may need to request access first.*

### Step 3: Set Permissions
1. **Choose** the appropriate permission level from the dropdown:
   - **View** - For people who just need to see what's there
   - **Edit** - For people who will add/remove items
   - **Admin** - For co-managers who need full control
2. **Consider** what level of access this person really needs

### Step 4: Complete the Share
1. **Click** "Share Container"
2. **Wait** for the success confirmation
3. **Done!** The person now has access to your container

## ğŸš¨ Common Issues and Solutions

### "User not found" Error
**Problem**: The email address doesn't match any approved user  
**Solution**: 
- Double-check the email address for typos
- Confirm the person has requested and been approved for Hearth access
- Ask them to check their approval status

### "User not approved" Error
**Problem**: The person exists but hasn't been approved yet  
**Solution**:
- The person needs to submit a registration request
- An admin needs to approve their request
- Try sharing again after they're approved

### "Already shared" Message
**Problem**: You're trying to share with someone who already has access  
**Solution**:
- Check the current shares list in the modal
- Update their permission level instead of creating a new share
- Use the "Update" button to change their access

### Can't See Share Button
**Problem**: The Share button isn't visible  
**Solution**:
- Confirm you own the container (not just have access to it)
- Check that you have Admin permission if it's a shared container
- Only container owners and Admin users can share containers

### Permission Denied Errors
**Problem**: Getting permission errors when trying to access features  
**Solution**:
- Check your permission level (View/Edit/Admin)
- Confirm the feature you're trying to use matches your permission level
- Contact the container owner if you need higher permissions

## ğŸ’¡ Best Practices

### Choosing Permission Levels
- **Start with View** - You can always upgrade permissions later
- **Use Edit sparingly** - Only for people who actively manage items
- **Reserve Admin** - For trusted co-managers only

### Managing Shared Containers
- **Regular reviews** - Periodically review who has access
- **Remove unused access** - Revoke access for people who no longer need it
- **Clear communication** - Let people know what level of access they have

### Security Considerations
- **Only share with trusted users** - Shared users can see all container contents
- **Monitor changes** - Keep track of who's making changes to shared containers
- **Use appropriate permissions** - Don't give more access than necessary
